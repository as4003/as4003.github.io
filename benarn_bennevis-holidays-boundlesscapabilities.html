    Attraction Sites in Scotland

    • GetYourGuide Link

      • These are a few of the Attraction Sights that can be visited:


    • Mountain / Hill Climbing: (Needs Advance Booking)

      • This is about getting ready to climb a mountain.
      • The notes for this can be seen in my Tips & Advice of Climbing.
      • There are many mountains/hills in Scotland
      • Exercise is a big requirement to be able to climb a mountain.
      • This can be made accessible by telling the guide about your ability.
      • The guides should be able to ensure, it is accessible.


    • Edingburgh Castle: Link (Needs Advance Booking for Guided Tours)

      • The Edinburgh Castle is a historic castle in Edinburgh, Scotland.
      • Stands on Castle Rock, which is being lived in by humans since at least the Iron Age.
      • The castle has been there since the reign of Malcolm III in the 11th century, as a royal residence.
      • This castle stopped being a royal residence in 1633.
      • The Edinburgh Castle is very accessible.
      • People can access the castle over a wooden-slatted bridge, which is fully wheelchair accessible.
      • There are cobbled-pathways and ramps to gain access to all the rooms in the castle..
      • This castle is fully accessible and very historically interesting.


    • National Museum of Scotland: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • This is very accessible.
      • The National Museum of Scotland is a museum of Scottish history and culture.
      • It has many collections of different national and international objects.
      • It was formed in 2006.
      • People can explore the diversity, world cultures, science and technology, art, and Scottish history.
      • The diverse collections take people on a journey of discovery through the history of Scotland.
      • The museum is wheelchair accessible, with the use of ramps and a lift.


    • Vaults: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • People can take a tour beneath the streets of Edinburgh's Old Town into a dimly lit space.
      • The vaults aren't wheelchair accessible because entry is possible by descending a single flight of spiral stairs.


    • Stonehenge: Link

      • Stonehenge is a prehistoric megalithic structure on Salisbury Plain.
      • Consists of an outer ring of vertical sarsen standing stones.
      • Each stone is around 13 feet high, seven feet wide, and weighing around 25 tons, topped by connecting horizontal lintel stones.
      • The Stonehenge is wheelchair accessible via tarmac and grass paths .
      • The Stonehenge is very historically interesting.


    • Palace of Holyrood: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • The Palace of Holyroodhouse stands at the end of Edinburgh's Royal Mile.
      • The Palace of Holyroodhouse is the official residence of the monarchy in Scotland.
      • It is the official residence of King Charles the 3rd when he visits Edinburgh.
      • The Palace of Holyrood has an impressive stone staircase, which was built for Charles II.
      • In the Palace, there is a life-size angel holds the Scottish crown.
      • The Palace of Holyroodhouse is the Official Residence of The Queen in Scotland, Mary, Queen of Scots, from 1542 to 1587.
      • The palace is fully wheelchair-accessible, with the use of a lift and extra benches everywhere.
      • This Palace of Holyroodhouse is historically interesting and has many different artifacts.


    • Edinburgh Zoo: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • The Edinburgh Zoo is home to the Royal Zoological Society of Scotland (RZSS).
      • Consists of more than 2,500 animals.
      • The Edinburgh Zoo is there to help people experience nature and learn about the challenges facing wildlife.
      • The zoo is wheelchair accessible via tarmac paths and the ramps.
      • The Edinburgh Zoo is very enviromentally interesting.
