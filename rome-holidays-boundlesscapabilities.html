    Attraction Sites in Rome

    • GetYourGuide Link

      • These are a few of the Attraction Sights that can be visited:


    • Colosseum: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • This is very accessible.
      • The pavement to enter the gate of the Colosseum is rocky.
      • There is a smooth walkway from the gate to enter the Colosseum.
      • The Roman Colosseum is in the center of Rome.
      • It is in the empire of the Ancient Europe.
      • The Colosseum is one of the most famous architectural and man-made monuments in the world.
      • It is a place to view gladiatorial arena and witness staged hunts.
      • The Colosseum is the largest amphithearter with it's long and rich history.
      • There have been several historical fires and other natural disasters.
      • The Roman Colosseum is made of travertine stone.
      • There is a lift to go up the platforms to get to view the different stages of the arena.
      • There are also many historical artifacts, which are very informative.
      • The Colosseum is very accessible with the use of ramps and a lift.


    • Fontana de Trevi: Link (Needs Advance Booking, if want to take a underground tour)

      • A 18th-century fountain in the Trevi district in Rome.
      • This isn't very accessible, since it is in a cobble-stoned area.
      • It was designed by several different people in 1762.
      • The Fountain has a intricate artwork decorated in the Baroque style.
      • The Trevi Fountain has a lot of historical information with detail attached to the fountain.
      • The statues on the Trevi Fountain are used to symbolize the fertility of earth.
      • The fountain has a range of taps, to insert water into the area around the artwork.
      • Tourists can throw coins into the water, with different myths.
      • The view of the fountain is very intriguing and fascinating.


    • Palatine Hill: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • The Palatine Hill is considered to be the birthplace of the Italian capital.
      • It is a combination of 7 ancient hills.
      • It is commonly referred to as "the first nucleus of the Roman Empire".
      • The area is quite wheelchair accessible, but it is a cobble-stoned area.
      • The Palatine can be seen from a distance but the walkway is rough.
      • Can go up the hill, although the path is very rocky and full of pebbles.
      • The Palatine hill is historically interesting.
      • There is a gate for the wheelchair to get out of the area, but it could need to be opened.


    • Roman Forum: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • The Roman Forum is the most important Forum in Ancient Rome.
      • It stands between the Palatine and Capitoline Hills in Rome.
      • The Roman Forum is known as the place where all the public meetings would happen.
      • It has been the center of religion and spiritual practices.
      • Most temples and artifacts are in the Roman Forum.
      • Surrounded by many public buildings.
      • The Roman Forum is not very accessible, since there is a long pebbled downhill towards the Forum.
      • The downhill is full of pebbles and not wheelchair accessible.


    • Pantheon Temple: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • This is exceptionally accessible.
      • The pavement to enter the gate of the Pantheon Temple is cobble-stoned.
      • There are a few steps to enter the Temple.
      • There is also a ramp to make the Temple be more wheelchair accessible.
      • Inside the Pantheon Temple, the entire flooring is smooth.
      • The Pantheon Temple is a former roman temple, that is the most preserved monument from Ancient Rome.
      • The Pantheo Temple is dedicated to the Gods.
      • It then became the burial ground for several important people, including famous artists and kings.
      • The Temple has a long and rich history.
      • There is also a large dome or hole in the top-center of the Pantheon Temple.
      • This Pantheon Temple is accessible and very historically interesting and informative.


    • Spanish Steps: Link

      • The Spanish Steps is a long climb of a steep slope of steps.
      • This is built between the Piazza di Spagna at the base and Piazza Trinit√† dei Monti.
      • It was designed by several different people between 1723 and 1726.
      • The Spanish Steps are used to lead to the French Monastry Church.
      • The Spanish Steps are not wheelchair accessible, since the wheelchair can't go down the steps.
      • The Spanish Steps have decorative flowers, along the side of the stairs.
      • The Steps are largely spaced between each stair, which means it is tough to climb.
      • There is a fountain that surrounds the Spanish Steps.


    • St. Peter's Basilica: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • The St. Peter's Basilica is the holiest sites of Christianity and Catholic Tradition.
      • it is traditionally the burial site of St. Peter.
      • St. Peter was the head of the twelve Apostles of Jesus.
      • St. Peter is also the first Bishop of Antioch and later the first Bishop of Rome, leading him to be the first pope.
      • There is a ramp to enter the St. Peter's Basilica, which ensures the area to be wheelchair accessible.
      • This place is historically interesting and very accessible.


    • Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • The Vatican Museums are the public museums of Vatican City.
      • The Vatican Museums have a large amount of different atwork displays.
      • The Museums have well-known Roman sculptures and important masterpieces of Renaissance art.
      • The Sistine Chapel is in the Apostolic Palace, which was the pope's official residence in Vatican City.
      • The Sistine Chapel is a commonly know Catholic Church.
      • This is very wheelchair accessible with the use of ramps and different stair lifts.
