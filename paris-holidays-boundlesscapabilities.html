    Attraction Sites in Paris

    • GetYourGuide Link

      • These are a few of the Attraction Sights that can be visited:


    • Eiffel Tower: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • This is fully accessible with a lift going from the bottom to the top.
      • Although the lift can be very crowded, it could be used by anybody.
      • There is enough space on each level of the tower.
      • On the first level, there is a seating area with a small cafe to have a cool drink.
      • The views are amazing.


    • Louvre Museum: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • The museum is very accessible.
      • There is a lift fit for many people to take people to the upper floor.
      • Guided on arrival of the museum.
      • A set of headphones and a audio-guide can be retrieved from the reception.
      • There are many ramps and a group of lifts in the area, which are accessible.
      • There is a large queue to view the Mona Lisa and it is difficult to find.
      • But there are many people to help.
      • There are some places that are quite inaccessible to a wheelchair user.
      • Because there is no lift access.
      • When accessing the Chapel, there is a chair lift which is quite terrifying.
      • Overall, it mostly is fully accessible.
      • The photos are very interesting and historical.


    • Seine River: Link (Needs Advance Booking to go on a River Cruise)

      • This cruise is quite accessible.
      • Once entering there is an entrance for the tickets.
      • The guide puts a ramp down for wheelchair users to access the boat.
      • Once on the boat, the top layer of seating is inaccessible.
      • Everything can be seen from the downstairs.
      • It is easy to navigate around on the boat to view all the architectures.
      • The sceneries are excellent and very interesting


    • Palace of Versailles: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • This palace is accessible.
      • At the entrance, there is a long walkway from the Honour Gate to the Royal Gate.
      • There are many ramps and a group of lifts in the area, which are accessible.
      • There are elevators at each level of the palace.
      • The restaurants have an easy level of access.
      • The palace is amazing and historically interesting.


    • Arc de Triomphe: Link (Needs Advance Booking to View the Interior Rooms)

      • Can be made accessible with the tours, where there are ramps.
      • Can be adaptable to fit requirements of other people with a disability.
      • Outside of the arc is fully accessible.
      • The sceneries look really great.
      • The architecture is very distinctly designed.


    • Saint Chapelle: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • Admission is free for people with disabilities and their companion.
      • There are adapted tours to help with needs of people with disabilities.
      • No accessible/disabled toilets.
      • The Chapelle is accessible with the ramps and lift to go to different floors.
      • There is a ramp to enter the ground floor.
      • There is a lift to go to the upper chapel.
      • The stained glass windows are amazing and historically interesting.


    • Montparnasse Tower: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • This building can be seen from the Eiffel Tower and is known as the "ugly building".
      • The best view are from the upstairs area, which can be accessed by the lift.
      • The viewing floor has many different sections of where to view the individual attractions.
      • There is also a small cafe in this area, up a few stairs.
      • The cafe is fully accessible with the small lift to get some food and a drink.
      • The tower is hard to find and look for the entrance.
      • The views are brilliant.


    • DisneyLand: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • The entrance has access for a person with a disability.
      • All the attractions and rides can be made accessible by installing adapted equipment.
      • The shows have dedicated spaces for people with disabilities and their carers.
      • All restaurants and shops are fully accessible.
      • DisneyLand is very accessible to many people.
