    Attraction Sites in Wales

    • GetYourGuide Link

      • These are a few of the Attraction Sights that can be visited:


    • Snowdonia National Park: Link

      • This can be accessible.
      • The national park is free.
      • Taking part in adventure courses requires booking and payment.
      • On maps, it is called Eryri National Park, located on the Snowdonia Mountain.
      • This National Park has brilliant landscapes attracting many people.
      • They can take walks and do many adventurous activities.
      • The sceneries and sights are beautiful.
      • The walks around the National Park can be very accessible.
      • The National Park has many castles and mountain ranges to visit and sightsee.
      • This is accessible and has beautiful scenery.


    • Caernarfon Castle: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • The Caernarfon Castle is a royal fortress-palace built on legends and bitter medieval conflict.
      • There is a lift to the King's Gate to ensure the monument is accessible.
      • Throughout the Castle, there are ramps and many lifts to climb the stairs.
      • This castle is accessible and historically interesting.


    • Conwy Town: Link

      • The Conwy Town is a Victorian Seaside Resort Town.
      • Can take a leisurely walk or travel up to the summit of the Great Orme for amazing views.
      • Conwy Town is a place to go on a seaside holiday with surrounding shops and cafes to have something to eat.
      • The Conwy Town is accessible since many surrounding ramps exist.


    • Mount Snowdonia / Brecon Beacons:

      • This is about getting ready to climb a mountain.
      • The notes for this can be seen in my Tips & Advice of Climbing.
      • Can sleep at a nearby accommodation to get ready to climb the mountain early.
      • Exercise is a big requirement to climb a mountain.
      • This can be made accessible by telling the guide about your ability.
      • The guides should be able to ensure it is accessible.


    • Wye Valley: Link

      • This is an area where people go to walk, cycle and canoe the River Wye.
      • Includes a lot of family-friendly outdoor adventures, including cycling, kayaking and walking.
      • There are many markets and historical landmarks.
      • There are many activities to do.
      • This can be made accessible for everyone.
