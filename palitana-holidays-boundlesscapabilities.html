Attraction Sites in India

  • GetYourGuide Link

    • These are a few of the Attraction Sights that can be visited:


  • Taj Mahal: Link (Needs Advance Booking to Visit)

    • This place is 90% wheelchair-accessible.
    • Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra.
    • Built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahān (reigned 1628–58).
    • To immortalize his wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died in childbirth in 1631.
    • It is a monument of love and a grieving emperor's ode to his beloved deceased queen.
    • Avoid showing skin, like arms, legs and cleavage.
    • Wear trousers, a long-sleeve top and socks.
    • Take off your shoes before entering the palace.
    • There is a gate at the entrance, where the view of the palace is amazing.
    • There is a path to the palace, with a piece of grassland around a lake in the middle.
    • The path is smooth and accessible, with a group of benches around the pathway.
    • There are a few steps leading up towards the palace, which aren't accessible.
    • Inside the palace is accessible since it is made of marble flooring.


  • Temples: Link (Needs Advance Booking to Visit)

    • There are many temples in India.
    • The temples can be historical or based on religions.
    • Temples are rich with culture, belief and history.
    • Festivals can be celebrated at different temples.
    • Most temples are historical and full of stepping stones from the outside.
    • Some temples have a smooth platform and may have built-in ramps for wheelchair access.
    • The temples can have rocky stairs or ramps towards the top.
    • Many tour guides or people help in the temples.


  • Beaches: (Needs Advance Booking to go to a Hotel)

    • Many beaches in the South of India surround the Indian Ocean.
    • Some of the notes for this can be seen in my Tips & Advice for Going On Holiday.
    • The beaches are sometimes inaccessible.
    • This is because of the rocky hills leading towards the beach.
    • The beach can either be a pebbled or a sandy beach, both inaccessible with wheels.
    • The wheels can get stuck in the sand or each of the pebbles.
    • A person can help to get to the sea/ocean.


  • Palitana: (Needs Advance Booking to go up)

    • This is located in Gujarat and is a pilgrimage centre for Jainism.
    • The Palitana temples are reached by a climb of 3500 stone steps along a hilly trail.
    • It is associated with the Jain legends and history.
    • Reaching the top involves climbing 3,700 stone steps, but it can be fully accessible with a dholi.
    • The dholi carriers can lift anyone down the stairs in a dholi to reach the temples.
    • The dholi carriers can be found anywhere on the stairs, with water to gain energy.
    • There are different paths and benches to have rest periods.
    • After the climb is completed, food is at the bottom.
