    Attraction Sites in Thailand

    • GetYourGuide Link

      • These are a few of the Attraction Sights that can be visited:


    • Floating Market: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • Large and well-known floating market among Thai and foreign tourists.
      • A floating market is where goods are sold from boats.
      • There are many row boats in the river of the floating market.
      • The Floating Market has many goods, including colourful desserts, fruits, and vegetables.
      • There are also many religious artefacts and different statues.
      • This can be very accessible with the help of the boat rider.
      • The Floating Market has many items for people to buy.


    • Chiang Mai: Link

      • Chiang Mai is a mountainous northern city in Thailand.
      • Chiang Mai has many historical temples, picturesque mountains, cool weather and scenic views.
      • Chiang Mai means “new city”.
      • The name ‘Parthenon’ means the house of the virgin.
      • This city isn’t accessible for wheelchair users since the pavements are uneven and cobble-stoned.
      • The city is very interesting, historical and has many amazing views.


    • Tiger Cave Temple: Link

      • It is also known as ‘Wat Tham Suea’.
      • The Tiger Cave Temple is a Buddhist temple which is a very sacred site.
      • The temple is known for the tiger paw prints in the cave.
      • The temple’s summit is at the top of a 1,237-step staircase.
      • It is a meditation spot for many Buddhist monks.
      • The temple isn’t very wheelchair accessible since there are many steps.
      • The Tiger Cave Temple has amazing and historically interesting views.


    • Emerald Pool: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • It is a tourist attraction in Khlong Thom Nuea, Thailand.
      • Mineral pool & popular swimming spot in a lush forest with a wooden walking track.
      • This area is accessible since the pathway is very smooth.


    • Elephant Sanctuary: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • The Elephant Sanctuaries help elephants with the ethical behaviour they provide.
      • They educate and promote sanctuaries where elephants are treated with respect.
      • This place helps to monitor the elephants’ behaviour.
      • People can come to feed, bathe and play with the elephants.
      • The tree houses are fully accessible with a lift to take people to the roof terrace.
      • The Sanctuary is wheelchair accessible since the pathways are smooth, and the elephants can move closer to you so you can meet them.
      • The Elephant Sanctuary has amazing views, and it is scientifically interesting to learn about elephants.
