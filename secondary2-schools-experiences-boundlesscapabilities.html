    How I Started

    • Closer to my home
    • My EHCP (Education, Health, Care Plan) Plan allowed me to attend the school
    • Well equipped school which provided good support with the help of the LSA (Learning Support Assistant)

    About

    • Much larger than my previous school
    • Ranges from Year 7 to Year 13
    • State school
    • Boys and Girls school
    • Catholic School
    • Secondary school
    • A lot of fields and buildings
    • About 9-10 classes each year in different places
    • Range of various subjects
    • Had to move around diverse classrooms

    My Experience

    • Started in Year 9
    • Left after Year 11, GCSEs
    • Used the Kaye Walker all the time
    • To improve my confidence and independence
    • All my classes were at ground level
    • Used the wheelchair for school trips and sometimes P.E.
    • Everyone talked to me, and I made a lot of friendships
    • Had Key LSA for all of my classes
      • #
    • Spent a lot of time in the institution unit (room 4), since it was my safe place where I met most of my friends from different age groups
    • Brought packed lunch to school every day
    • Played Boccia and basketball in P.E. lessons
    • For tests, I was allocated a scribe, reader and extra time

    Year 9

    • A Youth Connections representative advised me to follow a career in IT / Computer Science

    Year 10 & 11

    • Year 11 involved numerous exams in different subjects

    Prom

    • Apprehension on attending the prom but needed to overcome the fear
    • Excited to shop for the attire
    • Seating arrangements restricted me from dancing

    Reason Why I Left?

    • Left this school after Year 11, GCSE's
    • After a lot of research, I decided to attend a college to pursue a further education in IT
