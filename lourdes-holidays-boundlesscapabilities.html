    Attraction Sites in Lourdes

    • GetYourGuide Link

      • These are a few of the Attraction Sights that can be visited:


    • Massabielle Grotto: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • The Grotto is originally the place where Bernadette saw the Virgin Mary.
      • This is a place for prayer, trust, peace, respect, unity and silence.
      • There are many benches in the front of the Grotto, where people can pray and sit in silence.
      • The Grotto is a cave, where there is a statue of Saint Bernadette and a range of candles.
      • The Grotto is a historical and religious experience.
      • The area around the Grotto is very accessible, with the smooth flooring.


    • Lourdes Baths: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • People can go and drink at the spring, as well as wash themselves with holy water.
      • One of the apparitions were inspired for the creation of the baths near the Grotto, to occur.
      • A Spiritual journey at the sanctuary with Baths, taps, holy water, along with the Grotto rock and candlelights.
      • Water is one of the main symbols of the pilgrimage to the Sanctuary of Lourdes.
      • Overall, it mostly is fully accessible.
      • The Baths are very interesting and historical.


    • Torchlight Procession: Link

      • The Virgin Mary asked to take part in the Marian Torchlight procession.
      • This is a place for pilgrims to come to sight-see.
      • People can follow and carry the light of Christ to renew their faith in the procession.
      • There is a Cathedral with many candles and decorations, which is a great sight.
      • Many events can take place at the procession.
      • The viewing of the procession is wheelchair accessible.
      • The sceneries are excellent and very historically interesting


    • Old House of Saint Bernadette: Link (Needs Advance Booking)

      • This place has a lot of historical information about St. Bernadette's life.
      • The path is very rocky and pebbly around the individual rooms.
      • There are many ramps and pathways to make the area more accessible.
      • It is very amazing and historically interesting to learn about St. Bernadette.
